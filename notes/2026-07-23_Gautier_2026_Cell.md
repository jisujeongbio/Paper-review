## Why we selected this paper
- This study combines **snRNA-seq, snATAC-seq, and spatial transcriptomics**, making it useful for learning **multi-omics** integration.
- Most of my previous work has focused on RNA-seq only, and I wanted to learn more about the upstream regulatory mechanisms behind gene expression changes.
- I wanted to understand how chromatin accessibility and transcription factor activity can be linked with RNA-seq data to study disease-related cell-state changes.

## Main findings
1. snRNA-seq distinguished spinal motor neuron subtypes and identified a disease-associated state that emerged mainly in vulnerable alpha motor neurons during ALS progression.
2. These disease-associated motor neurons showed extensive changes in gene expression and chromatin accessibility.
3. Integrated snRNA-seq and snATAC-seq analyses identified transcription factor networks associated with the transition from healthy alpha motor neurons to the disease-associated state.
4. Spatial transcriptomics revealed early glial responses near motor neurons.
5. The disease-associated motor neuron (DM) signature was partly conserved in alpha motor neurons from human ALS postmortem spinal cord.
6. Differentially accessible regions in mouse alpha motor neurons were also enriched for human ALS genetic risk variants.

## Discussion notes
- The study integrated multiple omics datasets, including snRNA-seq, snATAC-seq, and spatial transcriptomics, to narrow down its findings toward the identification of a disease-associated motor neuron state. It was also useful for learning unfamiliar analytical approaches, such as CellOracle.
- The identification of a specific DM state that alpha motor neurons enter before cell death in ALS. Rather than simply showing motor neuron loss or damage, the study described the cellular and regulatory changes that occur during disease progression.
- (Eunho Kang) During the discussion, he also considered the relationship between SOD1 and oxidative stress, which has long been proposed as one of the major mechanisms involved in neurodegenerative disease. The study was interesting because it did not explain the findings solely in terms of oxidative stress, but instead defined a distinct disease-associated state in motor neurons carrying mutant SOD1.
- The analysis tracing early DM cells back to fast-firing, slow-firing, or intermediate motor neurons left some questions about the origin of the DM state. It might have been more informative to use approaches such as generative modeling, trajectory inference, or probabilistic mapping to estimate which pre-existing motor neuron subtype was most likely to give rise to the DM state.

## Personal notes
- In future ALS studies, it may be useful to focus on specific disease states such as DM rather than treating all motor neurons as a single population.
- The study identified spatial associations and potential communication between DM cells and reactive glia. Spatial transcriptomics could further characterize these relationships by comparing anatomical regions and defining local neighborhoods around DM cells.
